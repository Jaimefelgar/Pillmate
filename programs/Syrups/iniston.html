What it is?

Iniston Cough and Congestion is a combination of pseudoephedrine, dextromethorphan, and triprolidine. Pseudoephedrine acts as a nasal decongestant, dextromethorphan is a cough suppressant used to relieve non-productive cough (irritative or nervous cough), and triprolidine helps reduce nasal discharge.

Secondary Effects.

During the use of this product, the following adverse effects have been observed, although their exact frequency is unknown: Serious conditions affecting the blood vessels of the brain, known as Posterior Reversible Encephalopathy Syndrome (PRES) and Reversible Cerebral Vasoconstriction Syndrome (RCVS).

Stop using Iniston Mucus and Congestion immediately and seek urgent medical attention if you experience symptoms that may indicate PRES or RCVS. These include:

  • sudden and severe headache
  • feeling unwell
  • vomiting
  • confusion
  • seizures
  • vision changes


  • More common side effects: nervousness, restlessness, difficulty sleeping, anxiety, tremor, altered sense of taste.
  • Less common side effects: hyperactivity, overexcitability, dizziness and vertigo, headache, uncoordinated movements, dilated pupils, rapid heartbeat, high blood pressure, nausea, vomiting, bloody diarrhea, dermatitis, hives, skin rash, painful urination, increased sweating, paleness, and weakness.
  • Rare side effects: hypersensitivity reactions, angioedema (swelling of the face, lips, tongue, and/or throat with difficulty swallowing or breathing), agitation, anxiety, euphoria, hallucinations, nightmares, screaming and confusion in children. Drowsiness, irregular heartbeat or slow heart rate, abdominal pain, urinary retention, nervous sensations, fatigue, increased blood pressure. In very rare cases, heart attack, and at very high doses, seizures, confusion, and headache.
