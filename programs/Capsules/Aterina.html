Aterina

What it is?

ATERINA capsules contain sulodexide, which belongs to the group of medicines known as antithrombotics, used to prevent and treat blood clots (thrombi) that may form in blood vessels. ATERINA capsules are indicated for use in adults for: the treatment of chronic venous insufficiency

Secondary Effects.

Frequent side effects

  • Dizziness (spinning sensation)
  • Diarrhea
  • Upper abdominal pain
  • Skin rash

    • Uncommon side effects

  • Loss of consciousness
  • Headache
  • Stomach bleeding
  • Itchy rash
  • Itching, redness, and dry skin
  • Swelling, especially of the ankles and feet

    • Dose.

  • Treatment of chronic venous insufficiency: The recommended dose is two 15 mg capsules twice a day (a total of 4 capsules per day) for 3 months.
  • Chronic venous ulcer: It is recommended to start treatment with 60 mg per day via parenteral (ampoules) for 15-20 days and continue with the oral formulation by taking 2 capsules of 15 mg twice a day (4 capsules per day), with the dose possibly increasing up to a maximum of 3 capsules of 15 mg twice a day (6 capsules per day). The recommended treatment duration is 2-3 months.
  • Symptomatic treatment of intermittent claudication in peripheral arterial occlusive disease (stage II): It is recommended to start treatment with 60 mg per day via parenteral (ampoules) for 15-20 days and continue with the oral formulation by taking 2 capsules of 15 mg twice a day (4 capsules per day), with the dose possibly increasing up to a maximum of 3 capsules of 15 mg twice a day (6 capsules per day). The recommended treatment duration is 6 months.
