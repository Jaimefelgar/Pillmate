Paracetamol

What it is?

Paracetamol Pensa belongs to the group of medicines known as analgesics and antipyretics. This medication is indicated for the symptomatic treatment of moderate pain of any origin, such as: Postoperative and postpartum pain,Rheumatic pain (osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis), Lower back pain (lumbago), stiff neck (torticollis), sciatica, neuralgia, back pain,Muscle pain, menstrual pain, headache, and dental pain, Fever and discomfort associated with colds and flu

Secondary Effects.

Rare side effects

  • Discomfort
  • Low blood pressure
  • Increased liver enzyme levels

    • Very rare side effects

  • Allergic reactions
  • Low blood sugar (hypoglycemia)
  • Blood disorders
  • Liver and kidney disorders
  • Very rare cases of severe skin reactions have been reported

    • Unknown Frequency side effects

  • A serious condition called metabolic acidosis, which can make the blood more acidic, has been reported in patients with severe illness using paracetamol

    • Dose.

  • Use in adults and adolescents over 15 years of age: The usual dose is 1 tablet (1 g of paracetamol) 3 to 4 times a day. Each dose should be spaced at least 4 hours apart.Do not take more than 4 tablets (4 g) in 24 hours.
  • Use in patients with liver or kidney disease: They must consult their doctor before use.
  • Use in elderly patients: They should also consult their doctor..

    • If a lower dose than 1 g per intake is needed, other paracetamol formulations more suitable for precise dosing should be used.
