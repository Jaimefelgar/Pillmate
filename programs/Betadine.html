Betadine

What it is?

Povidone-iodine, the active ingredient in this medicine, is an antiseptic (it destroys germs that cause infections) that contains iodine. Betadine is indicated as a general-use skin antiseptic for minor wounds and superficial cuts, mild burns, or abrasions. In hospital settings, it is indicated as an antiseptic for surgical fields, puncture sites, minor wounds, mild burns, and surgical instruments.

Secondary Effects.

Like all medicines, this medicine can cause side effects, although not everyone experiences them.

In rare cases of skin irritation or allergy, stop the treatment and wash the affected area with water.

Rare side effects (which may affect between 1 and 10 out of every 10,000 people) include hypersensitivity and contact dermatitis, which presents as red skin, small blisters, and itching.

Very rare side effects (which may affect less than 1 out of every 10,000 people) have included anaphylactic reactions (severe allergic reaction), hyperthyroidism with tachycardia and agitation, and angioedema (swelling caused by allergy).

Other side effects of unknown frequency have included hypothyroidism, electrolyte imbalance, metabolic acidosis, acute kidney failure, abnormal blood osmolarity, exfoliative dermatitis, dry skin, skin discoloration, and chemical burns on the skin.

If you consider any of the side effects you are experiencing to be severe or if you notice any side effects not mentioned in this leaflet, please inform your doctor or pharmacist.

Dose.

Topical use. Clean and dry the affected area before applying the product. Repeat 2 to 3 times a day..
